Start here — three quick steps

Setup takes under a minute. The more days you log, the smarter Lum's patterns and predictions become.

1
Tell Lum your name & last periodOnboarding asks for an optional name, the start date of your last period, and your typical cycle & period length. Estimates are fine — Lum learns the real numbers as you log.
2
Pick what to enableAll optional, all off by default: an encrypted backup, and weather context. You can turn any of these on later in Settings.
3
Land on your Moon homeThe moon shows today's place in your cycle. Tap it (or the “Today's mood” card) to make your first log.
Tip
Logging just 10 seconds a day is enough. Consistency beats detail — even one tap (your flow or a mood) keeps your patterns accurate.
2

The three things you log

Everything in Lum is built from three simple daily logs. Add as much or as little as you like.

Cycle & flowLog period days as None / Light / Medium / Heavy. This is what powers predictions.
MoodPlace yourself on the mood moon, then add specific feeling words if you want.
SymptomsPick from a curated set (cramps, headache, fatigue…) and set an intensity from 1–5.

How a daily log flows

Each save writes only to your phone. You'll feel a gentle haptic tick when you pick, and a firmer one when you save.

3

The mood moon, explained

Lum maps mood on two axes at once — how pleasant you feel (left→right) and how energised you are (top→bottom). Touch the moon to place yourself; drag to fine-tune.

Get more from it
After choosing a quadrant, tap “Add specific words” to pick precise feelings (e.g. grateful, restless, foggy). These words make your Patterns far richer over time.

6

How predictions work

Lum uses a transparent calendar method — no black boxes. It learns your real average cycle length from the gaps between the period start-dates you log.

Please remember
Predictions and the fertile window are estimates from your logs, not medical advice and not contraception. Cycles vary. For health decisions, talk to a clinician.
7

Reminders that log for you

Turn on reminders in Settings → Notifications. They're fully on-device, and you can customise every word.

  • Three kinds, each its own card — a daily check-in, a “period coming soon” heads-up, and a “did your period start?” nudge. Turn each one on or off, and give each its own buttons & words.
  • Buttons that fit — the check-in offers mood buttons (tap to log how you feel), the period reminders offer Light / Medium / Heavy flow. Tap right from the notification to save, without opening the app.
  • Customise the text — edit the title & message for each reminder, with a live preview as you type. “Reset to default” restores one reminder anytime.
  • No double nudging — the daily check-in is skipped on days you've already logged.
  • Discreet mode — hide cycle wording & lock-screen content for total privacy in public.
  • Pinned so you don't miss it — by default the daily reminder stays put, so a stray “Clear all” can't wipe it; it clears itself the moment you log. Turn pinning off anytime in Settings → Notifications.
Try this
Set your daily reminder for a calm moment — bedtime works well. Then add the three flow buttons so logging your period is a single tap from the lock screen.

Optional weather context

Weather — temperature, barometric pressure, humidity and wind — can line up with how your body feels. Turn it on to let Patterns spot those links, and to see each day's weather in its detail view.

WhatHow it works
Off by defaultOpt in from onboarding or Settings → Weather. If you'd previously blocked location, Lum points you to the system settings to re-enable it.
Coarse locationOnly an approximate location is used — never precise, never background.
One online lookupThe only feature that uses the internet. Only a rough location is sent to the free Open-Meteo service — never your cycle data.
Context onlyIt adds Pattern insights; it never changes your period predictions.

Attach medical reports

Open any day (tap a date on the Almanac or the moon on Home) and attach a lab result, scan, doctor's note, or any PDF, image, text, or Word file.

  • Your copy is kept safe — the file is saved inside Lum, so it stays even if you delete the original from your phone.
  • Open or remove any report from that day's detail view.
  • Shared with your AI analysis — text reports are folded into the export, and PDFs/images/docs are sent along as files, so the AI can read them next to your data.

Your privacy, by design

Lum has no accounts, no servers, no ads, and no analytics. Your journal lives only on your phone.

App lockRequire fingerprint / face every time Lum opens. Settings → Privacy.
Quick exitTurn on a gesture in Settings → Privacy (shake, triple-tap, or three-finger swipe) to instantly hide Lum to the background. Off by default.
Hide in switcherBlanks Lum's preview in the recent-apps screen, so a glance can't reveal your data. On by default. Settings → Privacy.
No trackingNothing about you leaves your device — only the optional weather & backups you choose, plus a quick check for app updates (a public file, no data sent).
10

Keep your data safe — and yours

Because everything is on your phone, you control backups and exports.

A
Encrypted backup fileSettings → Backup. Save an encrypted file to any folder (including your own cloud drive). It's locked with a passphrase only you know — even if someone finds it, it's unreadable.
B
Back up to your Google DriveSettings → Google Drive. Sign in to your own Drive and Lum keeps an encrypted backup in a “Lum” folder you can open right from Settings. Lum can only touch the files it creates — never your other Drive files. Tick “Include medical reports” to bring your attachments along too.
C
Automatic backupSchedule a daily or weekly encrypted backup — to your folder and/or your Drive — quietly in the background. Settings shows the last backup time for each.
D
Export PDF / CSVGenerate a shareable PDF (great for a doctor's visit) or a CSV of your raw data, any time.
Don't lose your passphrase
Your backup passphrase can't be recovered — Lum never sees it. Store it somewhere safe, like a password manager.
11

Analyse your data with any AI ✨

This is the magic one. Export a special file, hand it to any AI — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — and it instantly reviews your history like a personal analyst. You don't type a single prompt.

No prompt needed
The file already contains the instructions. The moment you send it, the AI acts as a senior cycle-health analyst, finds your patterns, and shows you at least 10 insights automatically. You only type something if you want to dig deeper.

How it works

1
Open the exportSettings → “Export & analyse with AI”, then choose AI analysis ✨.
2
Pick a date rangeThis cycle, last 3, all of it — or a custom from/to range.
3
Share to your AI appSend the file in ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini. That's it.
4
Read your analysisPatterns across cycle length, mood, symptoms, sleep, weather — with the numbers. Ask follow-ups to go deeper.
File typeWhen to use
Text (.txt) ✓ recommendedEvery AI reads it perfectly, so the built-in instructions are always followed. The safest choice.
PDFFine for most AIs, but some can jumble table order when extracting text. Use text if unsure.

What the AI gives back

  • 10+ specific patterns, each backed by your real numbers — across cycle length & regularity, flow, mood, symptoms, sleep, energy and weather.
  • 30 relationships explored — the export asks the AI to work through a curated checklist (e.g. sleep vs cramps, pressure vs headaches, steps vs cravings).
  • Charts, if your AI can draw them — otherwise the same analysis in clear text and tables (it never refuses for lack of graphics).
  • A “do you need a doctor?” read — a calm, data-based view on whether something's worth raising with a clinician (it never diagnoses).
  • It offers you deeper questions — e.g. “Want me to dig into your periods vs headaches?” — so you can keep exploring by just picking one.
  • Your attached reports too — any medical reports on those days are included so the AI reads them alongside your data.
If your AI asks for a message
Some apps (like ChatGPT) want a line of text with an attachment. The export screen has a ready-made message with a Copy button — paste it in if needed. Otherwise the file alone is enough.
A gentle reminder
An AI gives general informational analysis, not medical advice. The exported file tells it to stay within those limits — but for health decisions, talk to a clinician.

Your first-week checklist

  • Log something every day — even one tap keeps patterns accurate.
  • Add specific mood words a few times so insights get richer.
  • Turn on a daily reminder at a time that suits you.
  • Set up an encrypted backup (a file or your Google Drive) and save the passphrase.
  • After ~2 cycles, check Patterns for your first insights.
  • Try the Analyse with AI export — hand your data to any AI for a deeper look.
