Privacy Policy

Your data, your phone.

Effective 2 June 2026 · Published by Nuvo Labs · getnuvolabs@gmail.com

The short version
Lum is a private, on-device health journal. There's no Lum account, no Lum server, no analytics, no ads, and no trackers. Everything you log stays on your phone, and we never see, collect, transmit, or sell your data. Two features are optional and off by default: Weather context (sends only an approximate location to a weather service, §7), and an encrypted backup to your own Google Drive (§6) — and even then, your backup is scrambled on your phone first, so it's unreadable to anyone but you.
1

Who we are

Lum is developed and published by Nuvo Labs. Reach us anytime at getnuvolabs@gmail.com.

2

Private by design

All information you enter — period and cycle data, moods, symptoms, notes, and any health metrics — is stored only on your device in the app's local database. There is no Lum account, no Lum server, and no cloud owned or operated by us. We have no ability to access your data.

3

What the app stores (on your device)

  • Cycle and period entries (dates, flow), and predictions derived from them.
  • Moods, symptoms, and free-text notes you choose to add.
  • An optional display name and your cycle baseline from onboarding.
  • App settings (e.g., app-lock, theme, notification and backup preferences).
  • Optionally, sleep & steps from Health Connect (§4) and local weather (§6).
  • Any medical reports you attach to a day — copied into Lum's private storage so they stay available even if you delete the original (see §4).

This never leaves your device unless you export it (§6), back it up to a file or your own Google Drive (§6), or turn on the optional Weather context (§7). Separately, Lum makes two small one-way requests to our public docs page that send none of your data — no account, no identifiers, no health data: (1) when you open this policy while online, it may quietly download the latest version of this policy text so you always see the current wording; and (2) on app open it checks a tiny public file to see whether a newer version of Lum is available, so it can let you know (and, rarely, ask you to update). Both simply fetch a public file — there is no SDK and nothing about you is sent.

4

Medical reports you attach

You can attach files (PDF, image, text, or Word documents) such as lab results or a doctor's note to a day. When you do, Lum copies the file into its own private storage so it remains available even if you delete the original from your phone. These copies live only on your device, are never uploaded by us, and are removed when you delete that attachment or erase all data (§10). If you choose the “Analyse with AI” export (§6), attachments for the selected days are included in what you share — that's your choice, in that moment.

5

Health Connect (optional)

If you connect Lum to Android Health Connect, the app reads your sleep and steps, read-only, solely to show them alongside your cycle on your device. Lum never writes to Health Connect and never transmits that data anywhere. Revoke access anytime in Settings. This complies with the Health Connect Permissions policy.

6

Backups & exports (you control these)

Encrypted backup fileEncrypted on-device with a passphrase only you hold (AES-256-GCM), saved to a location you choose — daily/weekly automatically, if you like. We never receive it and cannot decrypt it.
Back up to your Google DriveOptional. If you choose, you can sign in to your own Google Drive and Lum keeps the encrypted backup in a “Lum” folder you can see. Lum uses the narrow drive.file permission — it can only touch files it creates, never your other Drive files — and the backup is encrypted on your phone before upload, so Google and Lum only ever see scrambled data. You can include your attached medical reports if you want. Disconnect anytime.
Export PDF / CSV / AI fileExport and share via apps you choose. Once shared, that copy follows the app/service you sent it to.
7

Weather context & location (optional, off by default)

If you turn on Weather context, Lum uses your device's approximate ("coarse") location to look up local weather — temperature and pressure — so Patterns can surface possible links to your symptoms or mood.

  • What's sent, to whom: only an approximate location goes to Open-Meteo (a free weather service). Nothing else — no identifier, no account, and none of your cycle, mood, symptom, or health data.
  • Coarse, foreground-only: never precise, never background tracking.
  • Stored locally; the returned weather sits on your device with that day's entry.
  • Not used for predictions — only observational patterns.
  • Fully reversible — turn it off anytime, or deny the location permission.
8

Notifications (optional, on-device)

If you turn on reminders, Lum schedules them entirely on your device — no push server. You control the text, the quick-action buttons, and a Discreet mode that hides cycle wording and keeps content off your lock screen. Logging from a notification writes only to the local database.

9

What we do not do

  • No Lum account, and no sign-in is ever required. No advertising. No analytics or usage tracking. No crash reporting.
  • The only optional sign-in is to your own Google Drive, and only if you turn on Drive backup (§6) — your backup is encrypted before it leaves your phone, and Lum can't see your other Drive files.
  • We don't collect, receive, store, sell, or share your personal or health data. (The optional Weather context in §7 sends only an approximate location, never health data.)
  • No advertising, analytics, or tracking SDKs.
10

Security & deleting your data

Your data lives in the app's private storage, which other apps can't read. You can enable an in-app biometric lock, and backups are encrypted with your passphrase. Erase everything anytime via Settings → Delete all data; uninstalling also removes all on-device data. Because we never receive your data, there's nothing for us to delete on our side.

Children
Lum isn't designed for children under 13. Since the app keeps everything on your own device and we never collect any data ourselves, we don't receive any information from anyone — children included.
